About Kottayam

Kottayam is a city in the Indian state of Kerala, known for its rich literary traditions, lush backwaters, and rubber plantations. It's a place where nature and culture come together beautifully. The town holds a prominent place in Kerala’s educational and publishing history, being the first district to achieve 100% literacy in India.

History

Kottayam's history dates back to the ancient kingdom of Thekkumkoor, which was annexed by Travancore in the 18th century. It played a vital role in Kerala's political and cultural history, especially in the movements for social reform and the freedom struggle. The first Malayalam printing press was established here in 1821.

Culture

The culture of Kottayam is a blend of religious harmony, classical arts, and literary excellence. The city is a center for traditional art forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam. It also hosts several festivals, such as Onam, Vishu, and local temple festivals, celebrated with great enthusiasm.

Top Tourist Attractions

  • Kumarakom Backwaters – Famous for houseboats, bird sanctuary, and serene beauty.
  • Vagamon – A peaceful hill station with tea plantations, pine forests, and adventure sports.
  • Illikkal Kallu – A picturesque mountain peak offering panoramic views and trekking trails.
  • Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple – A centuries-old temple in the heart of Kottayam city.
  • Bay Island Driftwood Museum – A unique museum showcasing sculptures made from driftwood.
